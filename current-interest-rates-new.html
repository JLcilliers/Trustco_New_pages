What Affects Interest Rates Today?

Interest rates vary depending on a number of factors, such as the type of product, inflation, and the Fed's interest rates. Checking and savings rates are variable, meaning they can fluctuate often. Because of this, keeping up with the Fed's rate announcements is critical. Certificates of deposit (CDs), on the other hand, have a fixed rate.

Mortgage and loan rates have a few other additional factors in play, such as bond market trends and employment data. View Trustco Bank's current rates for different products by clicking through each tab below.

Frequently Asked Questions About Trustco Bank Rates

What does APY mean?
APY stands for Annual Percentage Yield. It reflects the total amount of interest you earn on a deposit account over one year, including the effect of compounding. A higher APY means more earnings on your balance. Trustco Bank displays APY on all deposit accounts so you can easily compare options.
What does APR mean on a mortgage or loan?
APR stands for Annual Percentage Rate. Unlike a simple interest rate, APR includes additional costs such as lender fees and other charges, giving you a more complete picture of what you'll pay over the life of the loan. Comparing APR across lenders is one of the best ways to evaluate the true cost of borrowing.
Does Trustco Bank charge points on mortgages?
No. Trustco Bank does not charge points on mortgage loans. Points are upfront fees some lenders charge to lower your interest rate, but at Trustco, the rate you see is the rate you get — with no additional point charges to the borrower.
How often does Trustco Bank update its interest rates?
Deposit rates and loan rates are subject to change at any time based on market conditions. Trustco Bank updates the rates on this page regularly to reflect current offerings. For the most up-to-date rate on a specific product, contact your local branch or call 800-670-3110.
What is the difference between a fixed-rate and adjustable-rate mortgage?
A fixed-rate mortgage locks in your interest rate for the entire loan term, so your monthly principal and interest payment stays the same. An adjustable-rate mortgage (ARM) offers a lower initial rate for a set period — typically 5 years on a Trustco 5/1 ARM — after which the rate may adjust based on market conditions.
What is the minimum deposit to open a CD at Trustco Bank?
A minimum deposit of $500 is required to open a Certificate of Deposit at Trustco Bank. Interest is accrued daily and credited monthly. A substantial penalty applies for early withdrawal.
Are Trustco Bank deposits FDIC insured?
Yes. All deposit accounts at Trustco Bank are FDIC insured up to the maximum amount allowed by law. This means your deposits are backed by the full faith and credit of the United States government.
What is a home equity credit line and how does it work?
A home equity credit line lets you borrow against the equity in your home as you need it, similar to a credit card but typically at a much lower interest rate. Trustco Bank offers both variable-rate and fixed-rate options, with financing up to 89.5% loan-to-value. Qualified borrowers can close through our Fast Track program in as few as 7 business days.
